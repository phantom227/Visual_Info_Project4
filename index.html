The Value Found in Homegrown Produce

Following the sudden outbreak of the COVID-19 pandemic that spread across the globe in the first quarter of 2020, multiple retail stores have had to shutdown once social distancing protocols came into effect. However due to civilians still needing access to food and supplements, only a minimial amount of essential retail was permitted to remain open. Unfortunately, due to the rising case of the virus there are those that don't feel comfortable with shopping for their essentials out of fear of being exposured through the simple act of touching products that have been held by others.

It would appear that the active pandemic has greatly inspired Canadians to invest more in finding alternative methods to gain access to food essentials. Studies have shown that it has become quite common for Canadians to make their own produce within their established residences. Whether it's through a garden in their backyard or constructed by using an indoor setup depending on their resources.

It has also been discovered that there can be quite a lot of value from developing homegrown produce. With the pandemic playing as a factor, the scale of farm-gate value trend has also been rising as more Canadians begin to practice this approach in gaining their food essentials. Although citizens may still need to visit essential retails to plant homegrown produce, this method provides an effective solution for those that prefer to be extra caution regarding their health and safety as well as potentially gaining value.
